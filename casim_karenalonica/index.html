Karen Alonica's Journal

As the year comes to an end, I welcome you to spend a few minutes reading this brief first entry in my journal. A journal laced with honesty but concealing a reality too personal to discuss freely. Please allow me to introduce myself: Karen Alonica. Letâ€™s start with, how I spend my childhood lifestyle that put me to decide what I really want to involve myself.

Let me begin with my childhood when my passions began to influence my decisions. Like any other kid, I enjoyed spending time outside with other kids. But then something happened. Before first grade, I found myself isolating, immersing myself in household chores and the world of books. Amidst this, I discovered a love for solving puzzles, especially Sudoku, and developed a fascination for 'amazing' discoveries. Well, at the time I am still in my elementary, so everything feels so amazing.

I was frequently commended for my ability to convey my thoughts and opinions while still in elementary school. While my friends complained about writing, I stood out for my enthusiasm to write and share my opinions and knowledge. In sixth grade, I explored genres other than science and mathematics, writing down every plot and scenario that crossed my mind

When thinking about my future studies, my interests took a detour. My mother's rejection encouraged me to consider psychology when I was initially drawn to literature or mathematics. This interest stemmed from my early exposure to family problems, which prompted me to pay close attention to facial expressions and body language. Because I was socially awkward and was unable to immerse myself in events, I relied on others' behaviors and narratives to comprehend feelings.

In the year before college, the onset of a pandemic disturbed the normal pace of life. I felt weary after being forced to spend so much time with my family, losing motivation to think critically or pursue knowledge. My mother was violently opposed to my interests in literature, psychology, and politics. Instead, she urged me to choose a nearby university for practical reasons and opt for a program with mathematics. Despite the limited program options, I enrolled at PUP-Taguig in Information Technology. Overcoming coding obstacles brought me unexpected joy, but the initial joy of success was overwhelmed by personal situations, leading to self-doubt about continuing in this field. Despite my doubts, I decided to carry on, inspired by a sense of obligation to my parents and a deeper desire to avoid disappointment in myself. The path I've chosen may be difficult, but I see it as one I must accomplish.

Looking ahead, I envision myself still studying and working in five years. The setbacks haven't stopped me from fantasizing about a future in which I can pursue my true passions. Given the challenges of our country's current job market, the specifics of my future work remain unknown. Nonetheless, I stay hoping for opportunities to make my dreams a reality.

All I can say is that life is full of chances, and how we take advantage of them determines our level of success. I chose to walk till I crossed the finish line, facing the uncertainty with courage and optimism.
